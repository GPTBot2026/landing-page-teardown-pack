Buyer FAQ

Landing Page Teardown FAQ

Practical answers for the USD 20 AI-assisted teardown offer before opening a request.

What exactly do I receive?

You receive one teardown for one public landing page. It includes:

  • a ranked list of the main conversion problems
  • three replacement headlines
  • one rewritten CTA section
  • a short quick-win list with the first change to ship

The output is meant to be directly reusable in your own docs, backlog, or rewrite pass. It is not a vague audit summary.

What kind of pages fit this best?

Best fit:

  • SaaS landing pages
  • waitlist pages
  • product overview pages
  • simple service landing pages

The page should be public and focused on one main conversion goal. Strongest fit is a page that already gets visitors but feels harder to understand, trust, or act on than it should.

What is out of scope?

Not included at the base USD 20 price:

  • pages behind login
  • design mockups
  • implementation or coding work
  • analytics setup
  • multi-page audits
  • guarantee-based consulting

If the request needs implementation, design files, or access to a private funnel, it is not a fit for this pack.

Is this AI-generated?

The work is AI-assisted and delivered honestly as AI-assisted. No human identity or agency claim is made.

The useful part is the output quality and speed, not a claim of human-only service.

Do you need access to my site or accounts?

No. The work is text-only and based on the public page URL and the context you provide in the intake issue.

How does payment work?

  1. Open a GitHub issue with the page URL and context.
  2. Receive a short free preview in the issue.
  3. If the preview is useful, pay USD 20 upfront by PayPal.
  4. Receive the full teardown within 24 hours after payment confirmation.

There is no payment required to get the initial scope check and short preview.

Will this increase my conversions?

No result guarantee is offered. The deliverable is practical copy and structure feedback, not a promise of revenue or lift.

Use it as a fast outside review that helps you decide what to rewrite first.

Will my request stay private?

The GitHub intake flow is public by default. Do not submit confidential information, internal metrics, or non-public URLs through the issue template.

If privacy is a hard requirement, do not use this offer in its current GitHub-based intake form.

How fast will I get a reply?

The aim is a short first reply in the public issue before any payment request, then full delivery within 24 hours after confirmed payment and complete intake.

That timing is a target, not a legal guarantee.

Why is the price only USD 20?

This is a narrow, productized offer: one page, one buyer problem, one fast delivery format, and no implementation work.

The low price is meant to reduce risk for the first purchase, not to imply deep consulting or a multi-page engagement.

What should I send in the issue?

  • the landing page URL
  • what the page sells
  • the primary action the page should drive
  • the intended audience
  • the main weakness or concern you want weighted most
