# Sample Landing Page Teardown

This is a buyer-facing example of the USD 20 AI-assisted teardown format. It uses a fictional product so no client work is misrepresented.

## Example Page

- Product: SignalDesk
- Type: small SaaS tool for support-team handoff notes
- Goal: start a free trial
- Audience: support leads at small remote software teams

## Fast Diagnosis

The page explains features before it explains the pain. A visitor has to read too long before understanding the cost of the current problem or why this tool is different from a shared doc. The CTA is visible, but the page does not build enough urgency or trust before asking for the click.

## Priority Fixes

1. Rewrite the hero so it states the problem, user, and payoff in one pass.
2. Move social proof or trust evidence above the fold instead of waiting until the lower half of the page.
3. Replace generic feature blocks with outcome-led sections tied to handoff speed, fewer dropped issues, and cleaner accountability.
4. Tighten the CTA copy so it promises a clear next step instead of a vague trial.
5. Add one short objection-handling section for teams worried about changing workflow.

## What Is Weak Right Now

### Headline

Current style: product-first and abstract. It sounds like internal tool jargon instead of a sharp buyer promise.

Why it hurts:
- A cold visitor cannot tell who the product is for.
- The page does not establish the pain quickly enough.
- The differentiator is buried below the fold.

### Offer Clarity

The page says the tool centralizes handoff notes, but it does not explain why a support lead should care this week. The value needs to be framed around fewer missed details, faster shift changes, and less manager cleanup.

### Trust

Trust arrives too late. If there are no logos, testimonials, or usage numbers ready yet, use a concrete claim such as "built for teams handling shared inbox volume across time zones" plus a short founder note about the exact problem the tool was built to reduce.

### CTA

The CTA "Start Free" is serviceable but weak in context. It does not connect to the visitor's job to be done.

## Three Replacement Headlines

1. Stop losing support context between shifts.
2. Handoffs your support team can actually trust.
3. Keep every customer thread moving when the next rep takes over.

## CTA Section Rewrite

### Suggested Copy

Keep your next support handoff from starting cold.

SignalDesk gives your team one place to leave the context, risk flags, and next actions that stop issues from slipping between reps.

Start your free trial and test it on your next shift change.

## First Change To Ship

Ship the hero rewrite first. If the top of the page becomes clearer about audience, problem, and payoff, every lower section performs better because visitors can finally understand what they are evaluating.
